Topic Modeling and Networks of Co-Occurring Terms in the Spring 2020 Core STS Learning Objectives

 

Moses Boudourides

New York University Abu Dhabi

 

The purpose here is to present the results of a data/network analysis of the text of the Learning Objectives of 15 Core STS courses taught at NYUAD in Spring 2020. To do so, we are trying to answer three rudimentary questions:

 

1.     What are the salient or important words in the text of these Learning Objectives and how can one retrieve them through automated text-mining of the corpora?

2.     What are the aggregate patterns, the relational adjacencies and the central foci that might emerge from the textual-semantic entanglement of these words in the Learning Objectives?  

3.     Can the overall structure, the common orientation and the shared objectives of the ensemble of the whole set of the Core STS courses be discerned through a computational NLP[1]-based analysis of the dataset of the Learning Objectives of these courses?

 

To answer these questions, we are employing the following standard computational techniques:

 

1.     Topic Modeling calculating important words (or keywords), called “terms,” in the corpora of the Learning Objectives and classifying them into a small number of certain interpretable “topics.”

2.     Networks of co-occurring terms, i.e., graphs among terms (derived by Topic Modeling) linked through their co-occurrences in the text of particular Learning Objectives.

3.     Community partition of networks of co-occurring terms into a number of discrete groups (“communities”) of terms which lump together existing structural similarities of these terms so that the resulting community assortment might disclose the common characteristics of the Learning Objectives of the Core STS courses program. 

 

1.     Topic Modeling

 

Inside the corpus of the Learning Objectives of 15 Core STS courses taught at NYUAD in Spring 2020, we were able to locate the following four topics of terms:

 

·      Urbanity,

·      History,

·      Science and

·      Revolution.

2.     Co-Occurring Networks of Topic Modeling Terms (TM)

 

Below is the graph of the 31 top TM terms. These terms are the nodes of a connected graph and they are linked together through 149 edges (co-occurrences among terms). The width of a link between two nodes is variable and it corresponds to the counted number of co-occurrences of a pair of terms in the text of the Learning Objectives. The diameter of the circle of a node is proportional to the degree of that node (which is the total number of textual co-occurrences of the corresponding term). Notice that central nodes (terms) are placed in the core (the center) of this graph, while the less frequently observed terms are positioned in the periphery of the graph.

 

Next, by grouping TM terms in each one of the Core STS courses, one can construct the graph of courses sharing common TM terms in their Learning Objectives so that one might be able to understand which courses are close to which other ones. Thus, the graph of courses sharing common TM terms is a weighted connected graph consisting of 15 nodes (courses) and 81 edges (pairs of courses sharing at least one TM term, with the edge weight being equal to the total number of shared terms between pairs of courses). Notice that edge weights are indicated by the width and the color opacity of the link joining a pair of nodes (courses).
